These months were attested by [[Bede]] in his work ''On Chronology'' written in the 8th century <ref>[http://www.wuffings.co.uk/OECalendar.htm The Old English Calendar]</ref>. His months are probably those as written in the [[Northumbria]]n dialect of [[Old English]] which he was familiar with. The months were so named after the moon; the [[new moon]] marking the end of an old month and start of a new month; the [[full moon]] occurring in the middle of the month, after which the month was named. # The months were divided into 3 "weeks" of ten days each. Because the ancient Egyptian year was almost a quarter of a day shorter than the solar year and stellar events "wandered" through the calendar, it is referred to as Annus Vagus or "Wandering Year".
