<math>|\zeta-\zeta_0|</math> from the roots <math>\zeta</math> to any point <math>\zeta_0</math> can be estimated from below and above, seeing <math>\zeta-\zeta_0</math> as zeroes of the polynomial <math>P(z+\zeta_0)</math>, whose coefficients are the [[Taylor expansion]] of <math>P(z)</math> at <math>z=\zeta_0.</math> We report the here the proof of the above bounds, which is short and elementary. Let <math>\zeta</math> be a root of the polynomial <math>\scriptstyle z^n+a_{n-1}z^{n-1}+\cdots+a_1z +a_0</math>; in order to prove the inequality <math>\scriptstyle |\zeta|\leq R_p </math> we can assume, of course, <math>\scriptstyle |\zeta|>1 </math>. Writing the equation as <math>\scriptstyle -\zeta^n=a_{n-1}\zeta^{n-1}+\cdots+a_1\zeta+a_0</math>, and using the [[Hölder's inequality]] we find <math>\scriptstyle |\zeta|^n\leq \|a\|_p \|(\zeta^{n-1},\cdots,\zeta, 1)\|_q </math>. Now, if <math>p=1</math>, this is <math>\scriptstyle|\zeta|^n\leq\|a\|_1\max\{|\zeta|^{n-1},\cdots,|\zeta|,1\} =\|a\|_1|\zeta|^{n-1} </math>, thus <math>\scriptstyle |\zeta|\leq \max\{1,\|a\|_\infty\}</math>. In the case <math>\scriptstyle 1<p\leq\infty </math>, taking into account the summation formula for a [[geometric progression]], we have :<math>|\zeta|^n\leq \|a\|_p \left(|\zeta|^{q(n-1)}+\cdots+|\zeta|^q +1\right)^{1/q}=\|a\|_p \left(\frac{|\zeta|^{qn}-1}{|\zeta|^q-1}\right)^{1/q}\leq\|a\|_p \left(\frac{|\zeta|^{qn}}{|\zeta|^q-1}\right)^{1/q},</math> thus <math>\scriptstyle|\zeta|^{nq}\leq \|a\|_p^q \frac{|\zeta|^{qn}}{|\zeta|^q-1}</math> and simplifying, <math>\scriptstyle|\zeta|^q\leq 1+\|a\|_p^q </math>.
