[[South East England]] and National Strategic Director for e-Social Science. == Background and Research == David De Roure was closely involved in the UK [[e-Science]] programme and is best known for the [[myExperiment]] social web site for researchers, the [[Semantic Grid]] initiative,<ref>{{cite journal|last=De Roure|first=David|coauthors=Nicholas Jennings and [[Nigel Shadbolt]]|title=The His personal research interests include [[e-Research]] and [[Computational musicology]] and his projects build on [[Semantic Web]], [[Web 2.0]] and [[Scientific workflow system]] technologies, characteristically focusing on the 'long tail' of researchers <ref>{{cite journal|last=De Roure|first=David|title=e-Science and the Web|year=2010|journal=Computer|volume=43|issue=5|doi=10.1109/MC.2010.133}}</ref> through adoption of user-centric methodologies.<ref>{{cite journal|last=De Roure|first=David|coauthors=[[Carole Goble]]|title=Software Design for Empowering Scientists|year=2009|journal=Software|volume=26|issue=1|doi=10.1109/MS.2009.22}}</ref> Prior to e-Science he worked in [[Distributed Computing]], [[Amorphous computing]], [[Ubiquitous computing]] and [[Hypertext]]. He is a Fellow of the [[British Computer Society]] (FBCS) and a member of the Scientific Council of the [[Web Science Trust]]. == Career == David Charles De Roure grew up in [[West Sussex]] and studied for an undergraduate degree in Mathematics with Physics at the [[University of Southampton]], where he also received his PhD in 1990 initially under the supervision of [[D.W. Barron]]. He held a longstanding position in the [[School of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton]] from its formation as a department in 1986, becoming a full professor in 2000. He was Warden of [[Wessex_Lane_Halls#South_Stoneham_House|South Stoneham House]] in the late 80s.
