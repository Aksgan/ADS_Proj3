Consider creating [[List of television shows featuring zombies]] if you think the show is that significant -->[[Image:THRILLERghouls.jpg|thumb|right|150px|A promotional photo from the ''[[Thriller (music video)|Thriller]]'' music video with the zombie backup dancers]] One of the most famous zombie-themed television appearances was 1983's ''[[Thriller (music video)|Thriller]]'', a [[Michael Jackson]] [[music video]], directed by [[John Landis]]. One of the most popular music videos of all time, it is a [[horror film]] [[parody]] featuring choreographed zombies performing with [[Michael Jackson|Jackson]]. Many pop culture media have paid tribute to this scene alone, including zombie films such as ''Return of the Living Dead 2'', cementing ''Thriller'''s place in zombie history. Fantasy-themed shows such as ''[[Buffy the Vampire Slayer (TV series)|Buffy the Vampire Slayer]]'', ''[[The X-Files]]'' sometimes include zombies as part of their horror/fantasy settings. Romero-styled zombie outbreaks are often featured in animated shows, such as in the Halloween episodes of ''[[The Simpsons]]'', ''[[South Park]]'', and ''[[Invader Zim]]''. In the far east, zombies also often appear in anime, such as ''[[Samurai Champloo]]'', ''[[Highschool of the Dead|Highschool Of the Dead]]'', ''[[Tokyo Majin Gakuen Kenpucho]]'', ''[[Yu-Gi-Oh! GX]]'', ''[[YuYu Hakusho]]''<ref>http://www.absoluteanime.com/yu_yu_hakusho/suzaku.htm</ref><ref>http://www.tv.com/Yu+Yu+Hakusho/Suzaku%2C+Leader+of+the+Beasts/episode/137982/recap.html</ref><ref>www.yyhworld.com/episode_view.php?ID=18 </ref>, ''[[Zombie-Loan]]'' and many others both within and beyond the horror genre. The [[Borg (Star Trek)|Borg]], a major antagonist race from ''[[Star Trek: The Next Generation]]'' and later Star Trek incarnations, exhibit zombie-like qualities such as having only basic instincts (to infect, or "assimilate" other beings into their collective), having an undead appearance (laced with cybernetic prosthetics), and moving in a slow, almost cumbersome manner.They also incorporate vampiric qualities, such as causing two puncture wounds on the necks of their victims, similar to [[Vampire]] bites and having a "Queen". In 2008, journalist/writer [[Charlie Brooker]] created ''[[Dead Set (TV series)|Dead Set]]'', a television miniseries wholly centered around the zombie apocalypse. The satire/horror storyline follows fictional ''[[Big Brother (UK)|Big Brother]]'' contestants and studio employees, trapped within the Big Brother house as zombies rampage outside. Zombies appeared in an episode of the science fiction series [[Andromeda (TV series)|Andromeda]], titled "Dance of the Mayflies". Zombies were featured in an episode of the Animal Planet TV series "Lost Tapes".
