The '''higher residuosity problem''' is one such problem. This problem is ''easier'' to solve than [[integer factorization]], so the assumption that this problem is hard to solve is ''stronger'' than the assumption that integer factorization is hard. ==Mathematical Background== If ''n'' is an [[integer]], then the integers [[Modular arithmetic|modulo]] ''n'' form a [[Ring (mathematics)|ring]]. If ''n''=''pq'' where ''p'' and ''q'' are [[Prime number|primes]], then the [[Chinese remainder theorem]] tells us that :<math>\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} \simeq \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}</math> The [[Unit (ring theory)|group of units]] of any ring form a [[Group (mathematics)|group]], and the group of units in <math>\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}</math> is traditionally denoted <math>(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}) ^*</math>. From the isomorphism above, we have :<math>(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^* \simeq (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^* \times (\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^*</math> as an isomorphism of ''groups''. Since ''p'' and ''q'' were assumed to be prime, the groups <math>(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*</math> and <math>(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^*</math> are [[Cyclic group|cyclic]] of orders ''p''-1 and ''q''-1 respectively. If ''d'' is a divisor of ''p''-1, then the set of ''d''th powers in <math>(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*</math> form a subgroup of [[Index of a subgroup|index]] ''d''. If gcd(''d'',''q''-1) = 1, then ''every'' element in <math>(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^*</math> is a ''d''th power, so the set of ''d''th powers in <math>(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^*</math> is also a subgroup of index ''d''. In general, if gcd(''d'',''q''-1) = ''g'', then there are (''q''-1)/(''g'') ''d''th powers in <math>(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})^*</math>, so the set of ''d''th powers in <math>(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^*</math> has index ''dg''.
