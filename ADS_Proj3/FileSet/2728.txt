There is, of course, no C-64 operating system support. ==References== {{reflist}} {{MOS Video/Sound}} [[Category:Graphics chips]] [[Category:Commodore 64]] [[it:MOS DVDC]]</text> </page> <page> <id>23025</id> <title>MOS Technology TED</title> <text>[[Image:7360 pinout.png|thumb|right|300px|TED pinout]] The '''7360 Text Editing Device''' ('''TED''') was an [[integrated circuit]] made by [[MOS Technology|MOS Technology, Inc.]] It was a [[Video Display Controller|video chip]] that also contained [[Sound chip|sound generation]] hardware, [[Dynamic random access memory|DRAM]] refresh circuitry, [[Programmable Interval Timer|interval timers]], and [[computer keyboard|keyboard]] input handling. It was designed for the [[Commodore International|Commodore]] [[Commodore Plus/4|Plus/4]] and [[Commodore 16|16]]. Packaging consisted of a [[JEDEC]]-standard 48-pin [[Dual in-line package|DIP]]. ==Video capabilities== The video capabilities provided by the TED were largely a subset of those in the [[MOS Technology VIC-II|VIC-II]]. The TED supported five video modes: * Text mode of 40×25 characters with 8×8 pixels * Multicolor text (4×8 pixels per character, double pixel width in x direction) * Extended background color mode (8×8 pixels per character) * Multicolor Graphics 160×200 pixels * Hi-Res Graphics 320×200 pixels These were largely unchanged from the corresponding VIC-II modes. However, the TED lacked the [[Sprite (computer science)|sprite]] capabilities of the VIC-II, making it unsuitable for most [[Action game|action]] [[Computer game|games]]. The TED did include one feature that the VIC-II lacked: [[luminance]] control. Fifteen of its 16 colors (black being the exception) could be assigned one of 8 luminance values, thus making the TED capable of displaying a far wider array of colors than the VIC-II.
