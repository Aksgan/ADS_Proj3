It was speculated that a complete redesign of the Xbox 360 hardware was being produced after pictures of a possible new motherboard design surfaced on {{date|2010-03-17|mdy}}.<ref>{{cite web|title=Xbox 360 Slim in the Works?|url=http://www.industrygamers.com/news/xbox-360-slim-in-the-works/|accessdate=May 1, 2010}}</ref> Ads later surfaced on {{date|2010-06-13|mdy}} showing a slimmer Xbox 360 design, which was expected to include a 250 GB hard drive and integrated Wi-Fi functionality.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kotaku.com/5562412/so-whats-inside-the-new-xbox-360 |title=So What's Inside The New Xbox 360? |publisher=Kotaku.com |date=2010-06-13 |accessdate=2010-11-11}}</ref> Xbox 360 S consoles feature redesigned internal architecture with the Valhalla motherboard, which allows for around 30% more space than previous motherboards,<ref name="insidenewxbox">{{cite web|url=http://www.anandtech.com/show/3774/welcome-to-valhalla-inside-the-new-250gb-xbox-360-slim|title=Inside the New Xbox 360 |author=Anand Lal Shimpi|authorlink=anandtech|date=2010-06-18|publisher=[[AnandTech]]|doi=|accessdate=2010-06-19}}</ref> and the [[XCGPU]], an integrated CPU/GPU/eDRAM chip using a 45 nm fabrication process.<ref name="engadgetnewxbox"/> This allows them to be both smaller and quieter than the previous versions of the Xbox 360. They also feature 5 standard [[USB 2.0]] ports (2 more than previous models) and an additional custom USB port for use with peripherals such as the [[Kinect]] sensor.<ref name="engadgetnewxbox"/> Unlike older models, 2.4 GHz [[IEEE 802.11|802.11 b/g/n]] Wi-Fi<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.engadget.com/2010/06/15/new-xbox-360-guide-microsofts-slim-console-explained/3|title=New Xbox 360 guide: Microsoft's slim console explained|publisher=[[Engadget]]|accessdate=2010-07-01|date=2010-06-15|quote=''Biggest news on the connectivity front is 802.11n WiFi built-in. The external hard disk drive connector has also been swapped for an internal bay for use with a proprietary hard drive; this is neither a standard drive nor compatible with older models. Other notable hardware changes include the use of one larger fan compared to the previous Xbox 360 models (which used two smaller ones) and the design of the vents, which are similar to those used on the original Xbox.
