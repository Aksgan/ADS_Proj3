{{Context|date=September 2009}} {{Intro missing|date=September 2009}} Let <math>E</math> be a locally compact separable metric space. We will denote by <math>\mathcal E</math> the [[Borel algebra|Borel subsets]] of <math>E</math>. Let <math>\Omega</math> be the space of right continuous maps from <math>[0,\infty)</math> to <math>E</math> that have left limits in <math>E</math>, and for each <math>t \in [0,\infty)</math>, denote by <math>X_t</math> the coordinate map at <math>t</math>; for each <math>\omega \in \Omega </math>, <math>X_t(\omega) \in E</math> is the value of <math>\omega</math> at <math>t</math>. We denote the universal completion of <math>\mathcal E</math> by <math>\mathcal E^*</math>. For each <math>t\in[0,\infty)</math>, let : <math> \mathcal F_t = \sigma\left\{ X_s^{-1}(B) : s\in[0,t], B \in \mathcal E\right\}, </math> : <math> \mathcal F_t^* = \sigma\left\{ X_s^{-1}(B) : s\in[0,t], B \in \mathcal E^*\right\}, </math> and then, let : <math> \mathcal F_\infty = \sigma\left\{ X_s^{-1}(B) : s\in[0,\infty), B \in \mathcal E\right\}, </math> : <math> \mathcal F_\infty^* = \sigma\left\{ X_s^{-1}(B) : s\in[0,\infty), B \in \mathcal E^*\right\}. </math> For each Borel measurable function <math> f </math> on <math> E</math>, define, for each <math>x \in E</math>, : <math> U^\alpha f(x) = \mathbf E^x\left[ \int_0^\infty e^{-\alpha t} f(X_t)\, dt \right]. </math> Since <math>P_tf(x) = \mathbf E^x\left[f(X_t)\right]</math> and the mapping given by <math>t \rightarrow X_t</math> is right continuous, we see that for any uniformly continuous function <math>f</math>, we have that the mapping given by <math>t \rightarrow P_tf(x)</math> is right continuous. Therefore, together with the monotone class theorem, one can show that for any universally measurable function <math>f</math>, the mapping given by <math>(t,x) \rightarrow P_tf(x)</math>, is jointly measurable, that is, <math>\mathcal B([0,\infty))\otimes \mathcal E^* </math> measurable, and subsequently, the mapping is also <math>\left(\mathcal B([0,\infty))\otimes \mathcal E^*\right)^{\lambda\otimes \mu}</math>-measurable for all finite measures <math>\lambda</math> on <math>\mathcal B([0,\infty))</math> and <math>\mu</math> on <math>\mathcal E^*</math>. Here, <math>\left(\mathcal B([0,\infty))\otimes \mathcal E^*\right)^{\lambda\otimes \mu}</math> is the completion of <math>\mathcal B([0,\infty))\otimes \mathcal E^*</math> with respect to the product measure <math>\lambda \otimes \mu</math>. Now, this shows that for any bounded universally measurable function <math>f</math> on <math>E</math>, the mapping <math>t\rightarrow P_tf(x)</math> is Lebeague measurable, and hence, for each <math>\alpha \in [0,\infty) </math>, one can define <center> <math> U^\alpha f(x) = \int_0^\infty e^{-\alpha t}P_tf(x) dt. </math> </center> There is enough joint measurability to check that <math>\{U^\alpha : \alpha \in (0,\infty) \}</math> is a Markov resolvent on <math>(E,\mathcal E^*)</math>, which uniquely associated with the Markovian semigroup <math>\{ P_t : t \in [0,\infty) \}</math>.
