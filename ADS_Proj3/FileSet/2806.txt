The playing field is made of up individual rows where cards can be placed; there are six rows for each player.<ref name="vcdiary" /> Cards are played by spending "resource points" which act as a form of [[Magic point#Synonyms|energy]]. The more powerful a card is, the more resource points are required to play it.<ref name="ignpspreview" /> Resource points are gained by specifically assigning any card in the hand to a resource row,<ref name="palgndsreview">{{cite web|url=http://palgn.com.au/article.php?id=8628&sid=7bc98aee1b422782a0db95fcbfff6fe8|title=Marvel Trading Card Game Review|last=Rositano|first=Joseph|date=2007-09-29|publisher=PALGN|accessdate=2009-03-27}}</ref> and only one card can be made into a resource each turn.<ref name="gspotpcreview" /> More powerful cards, such as [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]], require a higher available pool of resources and so can only be played in later turns in a match.<ref name="ignpspreview" /> Once put into play from the player's hand, a card can be used to provide a benefit to the player or to attack the opponent.<ref name="gradarpspreview">{{cite web|url=http://www.gamesradar.com/psp/review/marvel-trading-card/a-2007032013565591063|title=Marvel Trading Card|work=PSP|last=Bratcher|first=Eric|publisher=GamesRadar|accessdate=2009-03-27}}</ref> Types of cards include characters, such as [[Onslaught (comics)|Onslaught]] and [[Namor]];<ref name="ignpspreview" /> each character has an attack and defense rating that represents how much damage they can inflict and sustain.<ref name="gspotpcreview" /> Other cards include locations and equipment, which can be used to boost the statistics and abilities of characters,<ref name="gradarpspreview" /> and "plot twists" which encompass a wide range of effects, such as forcing an opponent to forfeit some of the cards in their hand.<ref name="gspotpcreview" /> Each player has a [[Health (gaming)|life bar]] that is depleted with each successful attack by an opponent, and a player [[Game over|loses the match]] when their life bar is empty.<ref name="gzpspreview" /> [[File:MTCGTeam.jpg|right|thumb|[[Lizard (comics)|Lizard]] and [[Electro (Marvel Comics)|Electro]] activating a "Team Attack". Both characters are members of the [[supervillain]] team [[Sinister Six]].]] Whenever a card in play is activated or otherwise used, such as resources, it is referred to as "[[List_of_Magic:_The_Gathering_keywords#Tap.2FUntap|tapped]]" and is generally unavailable for further use until the next turn. Unlike ''Magic'', however, characters can attack and defend in the same turn, unless they are "stunned" in combat. Damage is calculated by adding the difference between an attacking character's attack and the defending character's defense, plus the resource cost of the card. Any damage not absorbed by a defending character is applied to the player's life total.<ref name="gzpspreview">{{cite web|url=http://psp.gamezone.com/gzreviews/r29714.htm|title=Marvel Trading Card Game Review (PSP)|last=Aceinet|date=2007-03-20|publisher=GameZone|accessdate=2009-03-26}}</ref> The location of a card on the playing field can affect its statistics; in order to attack, characters further away from the opponent must possess a projectile weapon or the ability to fly, for instance.<ref name="gspotpcreview" /> Adjacent characters with a matching team affiliation within the [[Marvel Comics|Marvel]] universe can also reinforce one another's attack and defense,<ref name="palgndsreview" /> such as the [[Spider-Man and His Amazing Friends|Spider Friends]] and [[X-Men]].<ref name="gzpspreview" /> Cards activating an ability can be countered by the opponent,<ref name="gradarpspreview2">{{cite web|url=http://www.gamesradar.com/psp/review/marvel-trading-card/a-2007032013565591063/p-2|title=Marvel Trading Card (page 2)|last=Bratcher|first=Eric|publisher=GamesRadar|accessdate=2009-03-27}}</ref> and these abilities and counters combine to form a "chain" that dictates the order in which their effects are applied.<ref name="gzpspreview" /> ===Single-player=== ''Marvel Trading Card Game's'' [[Single-player video game|single-player]] mode allows the player to choose one of two different campaigns; one for [[Superhero|heroes]] and one for [[Supervillain|villains]].
